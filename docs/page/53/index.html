Accessing Amazon RDS Instances from your Desktop

#“Accessing Amazon RDS Instances from your Desktop” Whilst we are huge fans of the Amazon RDS database service (particularly the Multi-AZ setup), one thing that can be a problem is accessing your databases from the desktop. The standard trick of running an SSH tunnel to your EC2 instance doesn’t work here.  After much searching I found the following on an AWS discussion forum and it works perfectly: ssh-add YourKey.pem ssh -l <username> -L 33060:<ec2_rfc_ip>:3306 -N <ext_hostname> Then connect your local MySQL client to localhost, port 33060 <username> is the login user on the EC2 instance from where you normally access the RDS MySQL DB. »
